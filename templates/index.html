general notes (Simplified cause you know I'm not a qualified food scientist just a programmer)

Recommended Dietary Intake (RDA) is basically the minimum

Upper Limit (UL) is indeed the upper limit

Ignore sugar, cause the only information on recommended minimums and maximums on sugar I can find relate to ADDED sugars and not actual sugar that said though sugars from fruits, milk or whatever are finE for the most part, know your own body

magneisum data is weird, the limits on magnesium relates to supplementary sources, there should be no problem with overdosing if your magnesium sources are from food

you should not worry too much if you have a little bit of overdose on calcium and b3 just google the symptoms, if you actually notice them, than do something about it

dont worry about some of the weirder sounding nutrients (like molybdenum, linolelick my balls), the reason why theres no ratios on them, is because the food data was not available, likely because there aren't many cases of deficiencies for them. who tested the food nutrient

note potassium is however really important, however for some reason potassium food data is usually excluded so, might be ok if you're a bit below RDA

The RDA(minimum) and UL(maximum) for this nutrients are for fairly active males 19-30, if you're especially active you should have more omega 3, protein, calories, carbs, potassium(again note that data tend to be incomplete), vitamin D...
